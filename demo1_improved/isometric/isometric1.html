Eryn Bathke

Post Nature: Artifacts of Disintegration

×

Statements

As the curtain behind our modern lifestyle falls around us, revealing the devastating consequences of our actions on the environment, humanity is forced to reconcile with our collective role in the disintegration of the natural systems that sustain our planet. Yet, as we bear witness to mass extinction and fading landscapes, ...Expandwe continue to treat nature as a system that is external to us: a siloed system that we experience for pleasure, curate for visual aesthetic, and exploit for resources. This anthropocentric perspective and the resulting notion of separation is broken and leading us down a dark path to destruction. My work invites viewers to imagine a speculative future in which humanity is forced to live indoors due to the dire conditions of the outside world. With little to no access to nature, people are left with a romanticized view of the past, a profound sense of loss, and the weight of fault. As a result, ecological grief has become embedded within this new Post-nature era. As people learn to let go of the physicality of the natural world, they must cope by creating new digital, conceptual, and spiritual relationships with nature and embrace a new ecocentric perspective. Through a cautionary glimpse into a world that we could be in the process of creating, my work intends to call attention to the cause and effect relationship between the choices we make today and their implications tomorrow, and poses the question: when is it too late to change?
